Retirement Plans

Even in retirement, I hope to be employed, or doing volunteer work. Having “work to do” provides a lot of structure in one’s life (when to get up, eating at specific times).

In addition, by being of service to someone else, you gain useful transferable skills. After consistently taking long shifts at the Edmonton Food Bank, I can work efficiently for a longer period of time. This helps me succeed in school. Lastly, working lets one's efforts affect more than just themselves
